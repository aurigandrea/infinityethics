Digital Heritage Ethics Visualisation

This tool provides interactive visualisations of the ethical risks, best practices, and checklists from the INFINITY Digital Heritage Ethics Framework, helping developers understand and apply ethical principles in digital heritage projects.

How to use this tool

  1. Read the minimum viable checklist and assess whether all checks have been met. If they have not, proceed to the next step.
  2. Visit the Ethics Risks and Best Practices Tool. For each of the six principles (Participation and Inclusivity, Transparency, Accountability, Trustworthiness, Sustainability of Preservation, and Feasibility and Balance): read the associated risks. If you judge a risk to be medium or high for your project, check the associated best practices and checklists.
  3. The tool helps you identify the relevant checklist for the risks you indicate. Use this checklist as a starting point for discussion with colleagues and revisit it throughout the development process.
  4. Document the ethical decisions you make. Include this paradata in the documentation of any output.
  5. If the Ethics Tool and related checklist do not adequately address all the ethical risks associated with your project, undertake a full ethics review in discussion with the project leadership and consulting the Project Handbook, the Developers Rulebook, and the Data Management Plan for project-level solutions.

A quick checklist of the most critical ethical considerations for digital heritage projects. Use this as a starting point to identify potential ethical issues before diving into the full framework.
